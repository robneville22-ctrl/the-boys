# Jaegers 9U — Drill Library & Practice Reference

> Curated drills for competitive 9U travel baseball. Organized by category. Each drill includes time, equipment, and which players to focus on based on current team needs.

---

## Hitting Drills

### 1. Tee Work — Location Hitting (10 min)
**Equipment:** Tee, balls, net or fence
**How:** Set the tee at 3 positions — inside, middle, outside. Players take 5 swings at each location. Inside = pull side. Middle = up the middle. Outside = opposite field.
**Why:** Builds mechanics for all pitch locations without timing pressure.
**Focus players:** Mason (timing), Owen (contact), Kayson
**Source:** [Little League Hitting Drill Progression](https://www.littleleague.org/university/articles/hitting-drill-progression/)

### 2. Soft Toss — Timing Builder (10 min)
**Equipment:** Bucket of balls, net
**How:** Coach kneels to the side, tosses ball underhand into the zone. Hitter makes contact at the apex of the arc. 15 swings per player.
**Why:** Bridge between static tee and live pitching. Teaches tracking and timing.
**Focus players:** Mason (late swing), Alex (contact rate), Owen
**Source:** [GoRout Youth Hitting Drills](https://gorout.com/youth-baseball-hitting-drills/)

### 3. Two-Color Wiffle Ball — Decision Making (10 min)
**Equipment:** White and yellow wiffle balls (small size)
**How:** Coach front-tosses wiffle balls. White = swing. Yellow = take. Forces quick pitch recognition and swing decisions.
**Why:** Trains the brain to decide faster — swing or don't swing. Mimics game decision-making.
**Focus players:** Mason (frozen on called Ks), Porter (rarely swings — get him swinging at good ones)
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "Get two different colors, like a white and a yellow. If you throw a yellow one, it's like don't swing."

### 4. Small Wiffle Ball Hitting (10 min)
**Equipment:** Small wiffle balls (golf ball size)
**How:** Front toss with small wiffle balls. If they can hit the small ball, the real ball looks huge.
**Why:** Improves hand-eye coordination and bat path.
**Focus players:** Owen (struggling to make contact), Mason
**Source:** [Joker Mag - 9 Fun Hitting Drills for Youth](https://jokermag.com/hitting-drills-for-youth-baseball/)

### 5. Load-Step-Swing Rhythm Drill (5 min)
**Equipment:** Tee
**How:** Every rep at the tee: call out "LOAD" (hands back), "STEP" (front foot toward pitcher), "SWING." Make it rhythmic. Do it slow, then speed up.
**Why:** Builds repeatable timing sequence. Prevents rushing or being late.
**Focus players:** Mason (timing), all hitters as warmup
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "Every rep, even if you're at the tee, just imagine you're in the game. Load, step, swing."

### 6. Front Foot Direction Drill (5 min)
**Equipment:** Tee, piece of tape on ground
**How:** Put tape on the ground pointing from the batter's box toward the pitcher. Front foot must land ON the tape every swing. No stepping out.
**Why:** Fixes open/closed stepping that kills power and direction.
**Focus players:** Lasita (steps off-line), Grayson (sometimes steps off)
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "Step that front foot right to me. Everything comes right to me."

## Fielding Drills

### 7. Knee-Down Ground Balls — Glove Work (5 min)
**Equipment:** Balls
**How:** Players pair up 15 feet apart. One kneels on one knee with glove out, fingertips touching the ground. Partner rolls ground balls directly at the glove. 10 reps each.
**Why:** Isolates glove work without footwork. Teaches soft hands and keeping the glove low.
**Focus players:** All infielders, especially newer fielders
**Source:** [SkillShark Youth Baseball Drills](https://skillshark.com/blog/youth-baseball-drills)

### 8. Ground Ball Shufflers — Lateral Movement (10 min)
**Equipment:** Balls
**How:** Coach rolls ground balls left and right. Player shuffles laterally, stays low, fields cleanly, pops up, and throws to first. Emphasize staying low through the field.
**Why:** Teaches lateral movement and balance while staying low. Game-realistic.
**Focus players:** Zach (SS), Grayson (2B), Mason (2B)
**Source:** [GoRout Youth Practice Drills](https://gorout.com/youth-baseball-practice-drills/)

### 9. Short Hop Showdown (10 min)
**Equipment:** Balls
**How:** Coach throws short hops from 20 feet. Players field the bad hop, squeeze, and throw. Make it competitive — count clean fields vs errors.
**Why:** Builds quick reactions to choppy ground balls. The ball won't always come clean.
**Focus players:** All infielders
**Source:** [GoRout Youth Practice Drills](https://gorout.com/youth-baseball-practice-drills/)

### 10. First Base Squeeze Drill (10 min)
**Equipment:** Balls, first base
**How:** Infielders field ground balls and throw to first. First baseman focuses on: foot on bag, stretch toward the throw, SQUEEZE the ball. Count consecutive catches without a drop.
**Why:** Dropped balls at first cost us the game on 3/22. This is the #1 fielding priority.
**Focus players:** Lukas, Blake Fristoe (play 1B)
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "That dropped ball at first base was the difference in the game."

### 11. Backup Throw Drill (5 min)
**Equipment:** Balls
**How:** Catcher throws back to pitcher. SS and 2B must sprint to backup position behind the pitcher EVERY time. If ball gets past pitcher, someone is there. Rotate through.
**Why:** Team lost a run because nobody was backing up overthrows.
**Focus players:** Zach (SS), Grayson/Mason (2B)
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "Zach and Mason back up the pitcher EVERY TIME on a throwback."

### 12. Wide Receiver Drill — Fly Balls (10 min)
**Equipment:** Balls
**How:** Outfielders start facing the coach. Coach yells "GO" and points left or right. Player turns and sprints. Coach throws a fly ball over their shoulder. They track it and catch on the run.
**Why:** Teaches tracking fly balls while running. Named "wide receiver drill" because it mimics a football route.
**Focus players:** Crew (CF), Lasita (LF), Alex (CF/RF)
**Rob's note from 3/22:** Coach Andrew ran this with outfielders during warm-ups.

### 13. Infield Positioning Walk-Through (5 min)
**Equipment:** None
**How:** Walk all infielders to their correct depth: just behind the edge of the grass, NOT deep on the dirt. Show them the line. "One or two steps onto the dirt from the grass line."
**Why:** 9U ball doesn't come fast enough to need deep positioning. Playing shallow = easier throws, more outs.
**Focus players:** All infielders
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "They're not going to hit it that hard. Play up."

## Base Running Drills

### 14. Primary Lead & Steal Break (10 min)
**Equipment:** Bases, stopwatch
**How:** Set up 3 cones as "first bases" with 3 groups of 3 players. Coach acts as pitcher. On the pitch, players take their lead and break for second. Time them. Call out times to make it competitive.
**Why:** Gets 9 kids moving at once. Lots of reps in short time. Boys hustle harder when you yell times.
**Focus players:** Crew (needs bigger leads), Lukas (hesitating), Kayson (decision-making)
**Source:** [Dominate the Diamond — Base Running Drills](https://www.dominatethediamond.com/blog/team-baserunning-drills)

### 15. Secondary Lead Shuffle (10 min)
**Equipment:** Bases
**How:** Runner at second. On the pitch, take primary lead, then shuffle-shuffle for secondary. Feet should hit the ground as the ball crosses the plate. If ball bounces away from catcher → GO to third. If caught clean → shuffle back.
**Why:** This is the #1 base running issue from 3/22. Kids aren't taking aggressive enough secondaries.
**Focus players:** Lukas, Crew, Alex, Kayson
**Source:** [Little League — Secondary Lead Drill](https://www.littleleague.org/university/articles/backyard-tip-secondary-lead-base-running-drill/)
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "I need you taking a bigger secondary. If that bounces anywhere, I want you going."

### 16. Commit or Get Back (10 min)
**Equipment:** Bases
**How:** Runner at second or third. Coach simulates passed ball/wild pitch scenarios. Runner must either COMMIT to the next base or get BACK. No standing in no man's land. If you're stuck in between, you're out.
**Why:** Kayson got caught in no man's land on 3/22. Multiple kids hesitated.
**Focus players:** Kayson (#1 priority), Lukas, Crew
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "I do NOT want them doing that. He got caught in between."

### 17. Call Time After Sliding (5 min)
**Equipment:** Bases
**How:** Runners sprint and slide into a base. IMMEDIATELY after sliding, raise your hand and yell "TIME!" to the umpire. Every single time. Make it a habit.
**Why:** Kids are sliding in and getting caught in rundowns because they don't call time.
**Focus players:** Crew, Lukas, all runners
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "Crew, raise your hand and call time."

### 18. Aggressive Turn at First (5 min)
**Equipment:** Bases
**How:** Hitter swings (or mimics), sprints to first, takes a wide aggressive turn, reads the throw, decides: advance or dive back. Then steal second on next pitch.
**Why:** Teaches aggressive base running without being reckless.
**Source:** [Helpful Baseball Drills — Base Running](https://www.helpful-baseball-drills.com/baserunningdrills.html)

## Pitching Drills

### 19. Target Game — Points for Accuracy (10 min)
**Equipment:** Strike zone target/net, balls
**How:** Set up a target with quadrants (up-in, up-away, down-in, down-away). Pitcher throws to each quadrant. Award points: 3 for hitting the zone, 5 for hitting the target quadrant. Track scores.
**Why:** Makes accuracy fun and competitive. Helps find the right release point.
**Focus players:** Porter (walks too many), Owen (command), Kayson
**Source:** [GoRout Pitching Drills](https://gorout.com/baseball-pitching-drills/)

### 20. Balance Point Hold (5 min)
**Equipment:** None
**How:** Pitcher lifts to the balance point (leg up) and HOLDS for 3 seconds. Then delivers. Repeat 10 times.
**Why:** If you can hold the balance point, your delivery will be more consistent. Rushing kills accuracy.
**Focus players:** Owen (rushes), Porter
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "Owen, take a deep breath. Slow down."
**Source:** [Tommy Costello — Pitching Drills for Youth](https://www.tommycostello.com/article/pitching-drills-for-youth)

### 21. Long Toss Progression (10 min)
**Equipment:** Balls
**How:** Partners start at 30 feet, throw 5 balls, back up 10 feet. Repeat until reaching max comfortable distance. Come back in. Focus on arc and arm health, not velocity.
**Why:** Builds arm strength safely. Best thing a young pitcher can do for arm development.
**Frequency:** 3-4x per week. Every practice should include some form of long toss.
**Note:** "Focus on helping him throw farther and farther distances and feel comfortable throwing without thinking."
**Source:** [Driveline Baseball — Free Practice Guide](https://www.drivelinebaseball.com/practice-guide/)

### 22. Flat Ground Bullpen (10 min)
**Equipment:** Balls, catcher
**How:** Pitcher throws from flat ground (no mound) to catcher at full distance. Focus on mechanics without the added complexity of the mound slope.
**Why:** Saves arm stress while working on release point and accuracy.
**Focus players:** All pitchers, especially Owen and Kayson who need reps
**Source:** [Dan Blewett — Essential Youth Pitching Drills](https://danblewett.com/essential-pitching-drills-for-youth-baseball/)

## Catching Drills

### 23. Block and Replace (10 min)
**Equipment:** Balls, catcher gear
**How:** Coach throws balls in the dirt from 20 feet. Catcher drops to blocking position, keeps ball in front, then pops up and replaces to throwing position. 10 reps.
**Why:** Charlie and Zach need to keep balls in front. Runners are stealing at will (15 SB on 16 attempts against our catchers).
**Focus players:** Charlie (primary C), Zach (backup C), Grayson (learning C)

### 24. Quick Transfer & Throw to Second (10 min)
**Equipment:** Balls, catcher gear, bases
**How:** Coach pitches from short distance. Catcher receives, transfers to throwing hand, and fires to second base. Time the transfer. Make it competitive.
**Why:** CS rate is 6.25% — need to get that up. Faster transfers = more caught stealing.
**Focus players:** Charlie, Zach
**Rob's note from 3/22:** "Stay up in a squat. Don't drop down to your knees."

## Common 9U Coaching Mistakes to Avoid
1. **Over-coaching mechanics** — At 9U, rhythm and feel matter more than perfect form. Don't rebuild a kid's swing mid-season.
2. **Not enough reps** — Break into small groups. 3 stations of 4 players > 1 group of 12 standing around.
3. **Pitching too many innings** — Monitor pitch counts. Arm care at 9 is more important than winning a tournament.
4. **Ignoring base running** — It's the easiest way to create runs at 9U. Invest practice time here.
5. **Negativity from the dugout** — Kids absorb energy. Positive coaching = confident players.
6. **Playing to win vs. playing to develop** — Give every kid reps at multiple positions. Development > trophies.

## What Separates Competitive 9U from Rec Ball
- **Throwing and catching** — The team that plays catch the best wins. Period.
- **Steal game** — Aggressive, smart base running creates runs without even hitting.
- **Pitchers who throw strikes** — At 9U, walks kill you faster than hits.
- **Positive culture** — Kids who have fun play harder and develop faster.
- **Reps, reps, reps** — Small group stations, high repetitions, short attention windows.
